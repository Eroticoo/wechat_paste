a b s t r a c t
This paper presents an adaptive event-triggered controller design approach for a class of nonlinear 
systems subject to uncertain dynamics, unknown time-varying parameters, and external disturbances. 
co-design approach is developed, incorporating the candidate controllers, the event-triggered sampling 
mechanism based on the small-gain condition and the вҖҳвҖҳpre-routed searchвҖҷвҖҷ switching logic, such that 
the closed-loop switched system is practically stable with average dwell-time switching behavior. 
Furthermore, it shows that the closed-loop system can be exponentially stabilized by employing 
a dynamic event-triggered sampling mechanism if the plant is disturbance-free and the unknown 
parameters satisfy the exact-matching condition. In the above two cases, the infinitely many samplings 
are excluded in any finite time interval.

with nonlinear parameterization further increases difficulties to 
the adaptive event-triggered control. Our proposed switching-
based approach decouples the adaptive event-triggered control 
problem into three sub-problems: switching logic to supervise 
the candidate controllers, robust design of candidate controllers, 
and event-triggered sampling mechanism design. This structure 
enables the integration of existing stabilizing control methods 
into the proposed framework while maintaining robustness to the 
time-varying parametric uncertainty and the sampling errors.
It is worth noting that adaptive control of nonlinear sys-
tems with time-varying parameters has attracted considerable 
research effort over the past decades, but has attracted little 
attention from the adaptive event-triggered control community. 
The development of adaptive control with time-varying param-
eters ranges from the early works (Kreisselmerer, 1986; Marino 
& Tomei, 1999; Zhivoglyadov et al., 2001) to the state-of-the-art 
advancements (Chen & Astolfi, 2021; Sung et al., 2022; Zhu et al., 
2013a, 2013b). Specifically, Zhivoglyadov et al. (2001) and Sung 
et al. (2022) use logic-based switching to deal with slowly varying 
parameters, Zhu et al. (2013a, 2013b) proposed a modular-based 
approach to constructive control design and removed the restric-
tion of parameterвҖҷs slow variation, and Chen and Astolfi (2021) 
developed a congelation of variables approach to handle the 
unknown time-varying parameters.
To solve the aforementioned problem, in this paper, we first 
present a base scenario of fixed unknown parameter (Section 3), 
in which the three design components are introduced, includ-
ing a bank of input-to-state stable (ISS) (Sontag & Wang, 1995) 
candidate controllers, event-triggered sampling mechanism, and 
a pre-routed search switching logic (Angeli & Mosca, 2004a; 
MГҘrtenson, 1985). Inspired by the base scenario, a co-design 
approach is developed for these three components to handle the 
time-varying parametric uncertainty (Section 4), in which the 
sampling mechanism and the switching logic are simultaneously 
designed with the bridge of candidate controllers (Remark 8). 
Our proposed design guarantees that the plant state converges 
to an adjustable forward invariant set containing the origin and 
excludes infinitely many sampling events within any finite time 
interval. Moreover, we provide an extension of the main re-
sult which develops a dynamic sampling mechanism, such that 
the plant state exponentially converges, under the parameter 
exact matching condition (Section 5). In the extension result, 
the co-design approach is further emphasized by simultaneously 
selecting parameters from each design component.

6. Conclusions
This paper has developed an adaptive event-triggered con-
trol strategy for a class of nonlinear systems with time-varying 
parametric uncertainty. A co-design approach of the candidate 
controllers, the event-triggered sampling mechanism, and the 
switching logic has been proposed to guarantee the robust-
ness to the sampling error and the adaptability to the unknown 
time-varying parameters, such that the plant state ultimately
converges to an adjustable forward invariant set, the intervals 
subject to sampling and switching have been proved to be strictly 
positive. As an extension, a dynamic event-triggered sampling 
mechanism has been designed to guarantee the exponential con-
vergence of the plant state, when the disturbance is free and the 
unknown parameter satisfies exact-matching condition. Future 
research will be directed at establishing specific constructive 
controller design methods for several classes of nonlinear plants 
based on the proposed adaptive event-triggered control frame-
work in this paper. Another future research is to relax the exact 
matching condition in Section 5, while still guaranteeing the 
exponential convergence of the state.
